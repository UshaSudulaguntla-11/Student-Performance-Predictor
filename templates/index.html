Academic Success Drivers Report

1 Attendance is the Foundation

An attendance rate increase from 60% to 100% generates a massive predicted score gain of +7.91 points. In class attendance accounts for 38% of overall student performance variation. Absenteeism isolates students from curriculum context and tutoring reinforcement.

2 Weekly Study Time Yields Large Returns

Every single hour dedicated to studying boosts exam scores by +0.29 points. Moving from a low weekly study rate (5 hours) to a highly focused study schedule (30 hours) returns an estimated +7.2 points. Combining study habits with high attendance offers the highest compounded grade outcome.

3 The Direct Value of Tutoring & Support

Enrolling a student in tutoring sessions has a highly significant linear coefficient of +0.50 points per session. A full tutoring schedule of 8 sessions per month contributes an estimated +4.0 points, serving as an effective intervention strategy for struggling learners.

4 Internet & Resources Boost Equity

Providing internet access to a student adds +0.97 points, which represents the highest digital factor in the model. Additionally, access to learning resources represents a significant gap—students with low resources face a penalty of -0.40 points relative to high resource access.
